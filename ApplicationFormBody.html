What is an Application Form?

A template that employers ask their candidates to fill out to help them assess the skill set the candidate has.

  • Many employers use printed job application forms for specific reasons such as to:
    • standardize the information they gather from every applicant,
    • obtain information you might not include in a résumé,
    • test your ability to follow instructions,
    • assess your attention to detail, such as neatness, spelling and grammar, and
    • create a record of your employment history that includes your signature
  • TIPS
    • To a job interview, you must always:
      • Bring several copies of your résumé
      • Bring several erasable blue or black ink pen
      • Bring a skills portfolio
      • Dress professionally
    • Ask for two copies of the application form so you can use one for a draft and one for a final copy.
    • Read the form carefully and follow the instructions.
    • Write clearly and neatly.
    • Answer all questions truthfully.
      • If some questions don't apply to you, write "not applicable" or "N/A."
      • Never answer "refer to my resumé."
    • Avoid detailed negative answers.
      • For example, if you were let go from a job and you're asked to give reasons for leaving, make a brief statement such as "job ended" and then be ready to provide a complete answer in an interview.
    • List your most relevant education and training.
      • Include the dates you attended programs, names of the schools and the certificates or diplomas you earned.
      • Include training courses, special awards and volunteer positions you've held, especially if they relate to the type of work you-re applying for.
    • Give a range or say you're open to negotiation if you're asked to provide past salary amounts or current salary expectations.
    • You should always try to fill out the "Additional Comments" section.
      • This is your chance to provide extra information about your skills, strengths, and accomplishments.
    • Double-check the form before you submit it.
      • Make sure your spelling and grammar are correct.
      • Make sure your answers are complete and the overall appearance of the form makes a good impression.
      • Remember to sign the application form!
    • Attach your résumé to the application form
      • Do NOT attach copies of your grades, performance appraisals, or letters of recommendation unless the employer specifically asks you to do so!
    • Deliver the form in the way the employer prefers.
      • If the employer wants it delivered by hand, be sure to give the form by hand.
      • If you are not sure, just ask!

Definition of a Résumé:

A résumé is a document that explains a person's qualifications, education, previous experiences, and other valuable information to acquire a job.

How to Write a Résumé:

  • The Heading
    • Your full name.
    • Your Address (including your street, city, state, and zip code).
    • Email Address (don't use your work email because your current company will find out and may fire you!).
    • Telephone Number (be sure to have a professional voicemail message for missed calls).
  • Profile or Objective
    • This will give your employer an overview of your qualifications.
    • This focuses on what employers are seeking rather than what you are seeking.
  • Summary of Qualifications
    • This should include your skills, abilities, and experience.
    • Mention any notable accomplishments that you have.
    • Briefly explain why your qualifications for this job.
  • Your Experience
    • List the jobs and internships you have held in reverse chronological order, with the most recent positions first.
    • For each position, include: job title, company, location, dates of employment, and a bulleted list of the strongest.
    • The verb tense should be present tense for your current job if you are employed, and past tense for prior employment.
  • Volunteer work
    • Include only if the volunteer work caused an employment gap.
    • Explain why you volunteered and what made you motivated to volunteer.
  • Education
    • List your degrees: most recent and advanced must be first!
    • Include the name of school, the degree earned, and the date of graduation.
    • GPA (grade point average).
  • Personal Interests
    • List any interests if they are strongly related to the position you are applying for.
    • This section is very helpful for jobs where you do not have a lot of related work experience.
  • Skills
    • Present skills that are directly related to the job.
    • Employers typically list the required or preferred skills in job openings.
    • Use a bulleted list format when you list your skills here.
    • Most notable skills should be at the beginnning

Choose a Résumé Type:

There are four types of résumés you can use: chronological, functional, combination, and targeted.

  • Chronological Résumé
    • This lists jobs by reverse chronological order and gives a brief explanation of each job.
    • This is easy for employers to see what jobs you have held and when you had these jobs.
    • This is the best type of résumé to use when you have an extensive work history.
  • Functional Résumé
    • This is used by job seekers who plan to change careers, have gaps in their employment history, or their work history is not related to the job they are applying for.
    • This is the best type of résumé to use if you want to emphasize your abilities.
  • Targeted Résumé
    • This focuses on a specific job opening.
    • This also highlights any relevant skills and experiences you have.
    • This is the best type of résumé to use if you want to show that you are a good fit for the position.
  • Combination Résumé
    • This emphasizes your skill set and includes your work history.
    • This lists your skills and experiences first and progresses to your employment history in chronological order.
    • This is the best type of résumé to use if you want to show that you are a well-rounded candidate for the job you are applying for.

Tips for Writing a Notable Résumé:

  • Font and Size
    • Keep style features such as italics, underlining, boldface, and bullets to a minimum.
    • Use a font and font size that is legible and leaves enough white space for easy reading.
  • Résumé Templates
    • If you want, use a résumé template
    • Personalize it to make it unique to you!
  • Résumé Keywords
    • Did you know: companies use software to search for specific keywords in your résumé they need in job openings?
    • Sound mature and do NOT use contractions or slang terms!
  • Simple Mistakes
    • Use Grammarly, an online grammar assistant, to help you find any grammar or spelling mistakes
    • Make sure to proofread your résumé several times before submitting it
